Introduction

XCSoar is a tactical glide computer originally developed for the Pocket PC platform. In 2005, the originally commercial software was given to the open-source community for further development and has constantly been improved since. It is now a multi-platform application that currently runs on Windows, Windows Mobile, Unix and soon also on Android devices.

Highlights of XCSoar's interface

  • Moving Map
    The primary display, easily configurable to provide the desired level of detail.

  • Terrain Display
    Terrain and Topology data from all over the world can be displayed on the moving map.

  • FLARM Integration
    All received FLARM traffic is displayed on the map and on the inset radar screen. (including climb speed)
